Tout Bout finalists: Cast your vote by July 26

We received dozens of nominations, and now we have the top ten finalists in DFM’s monthly Tout Bout. The top ten were selected based on whether they were produced with the app, relevance/news value, production quality and creativity. Now it’s up to you to decide which Tout deserves the $200 prize. Review our talented finalists, cast your vote at the bottom, and share your vote with the hashtag #ToutBout. The deadline is Friday, July 26.

Gina Dvorak and Canan Tasci, San Gabriel Valley Tribune
“This Tout works on so many levels. It’s newsworthy. Los Angeles News Group ran a story about a hoax going on at In-N-Out burger, a Southern California chain with a world-famous cult following. After a fake burger went viral, we reported on how the hoax grew. Then writer Canan Tasci took it to the next level by building a real version of the fake burger. Gina Dvorak may have shot the video; the link is to her Tout account. Tasci has a lot of fun and provides informative instructions on a hot subject in Southern California.”
- Christopher Ledermuller

Mark Jaffe, The Denver Post
“These touts (7 integrated bits) were part of a story package and thanks to our Denver Post Online team were included in a widget along with the text story and some video we acquired.”
- Mark Jaffe

Natalie Alund, Oakland Tribune/Mercury News
“I shot this Tout on a plane from LAX to SFO one day after being rerouted back to LAX the day Flight 214 crashed in San Francisco. It followed a series of Touts I shot regarding passengers including myself dealing with chaos, fear and uncertainty. The day of the accident, the same plane I was on descended into SFO just minutes before the crash, jerked left and the pilot announced he was turning the plane around to head back to LAX because of the crash. This Tout shows relief of those same passengers who were able to make it finally back safely to SFO the day after the devastating crash.”
- Natalie Alund

Shmuel Thaler, Santa Cruz Sentinel
“I am nominating this Tout because the photographer used excellent judgement in picking a Tout subject. He didn’t shoot people sitting in a room talking. He was looking for the action. He opened with narration, which is harder to get people to do than one might think, and he got up close and under a jump. Plus, the finished product ended up being something that added to the story. You can have a still photo of an X Games competitor frozen in the air, or write about how the wind whips past him, but the video gave the readers a quick shot of exactly what it looks like as the rider completes a chain of jumps.”
- Christina Gullickson

Brandie Kessler, York Daily Record/Sunday News
“This Tout exemplifies the usefulness of the tool.It was pure happenstance that I bumped into a member of Pershing’s Own, the U.S. Army Band, while I was leaving the York Daily Record’s satellite office in Gettysburg to cover a Gettysburg 150th event. The band was in Gettysburg to perform for the anniversary. Staff Sgt. Pamela Terry was sitting near the door when I was on my way out, and asked if I had a cell phone charger she could borrow. She was in uniform and I immediately recognized her as a member of Pershing’s Own (I wrote about them before during the Presidential Inauguration earlier this year.)

As we walked toward the satellite office so I could lend her a charger, I asked her what instrument she played. Turns out she was a vocalist. Actually, she is a soloist! I thought it would be so cool for her to sing something quick for us at the satellite office. She agreed! Beautiful voice, don’t you think?

Without Tout, and the way it’s changed my thinking about getting video out to readers ASAP, this glorious tune would not have been. I know it’s not breaking news, nor did it require any editing, but it’s gorgeous. I still get chills listening to her sing, just like I did when I heard it in person. I’m glad others can enjoy this clip, too, thanks to Tout.”
- Brandie Kessler

Jane Tyska, The Oakland Tribune
“When BART trains stopped running in the Bay Area during the recent strike, impacting thousands of commuters, this story became a talker. Jane brought to life the sights and sounds of the BART strike protest at the Lake Merritt station in Oakland, editing in real time in the phone app and using a variety of different shots to keep the viewer engaged. She was able to then quickly upload to Tout, Twitter and Facebook, all in the space of about one minute. This Tout should win for it’s creative camera angles and great editing. Thanks for your vote!”
- Jane Tyska

Samantha Dellinger, York Daily Record
“This Tout was part of a multi platform effort that began with a printed piece in the newspaper. And then was promoted using Tout that pushed it out via Twitter, Facebook, Pinterest and also posted it onto my art blog using the Tout Widget. I also engaged our readers by including the name drawing for the winner at the end of my Tout video. A previous Tout video was posted to initially to help promote the contest too. This video was shot using Tout only.”
- Samantha Dellinger

Sonya Paclob, York Daily Record
“Checking video gear before going out on assignment is crucial. There are so many components and so I did a creative behind the scenes of the York Daily Record newsroom.”
- Sonya Paclob

Fred Robledo, San Gabriel Valley Tribune
“Since this is the inaugural contest, I’m nominating a Tout video from high school’s March Madness in which I captured a dramatic buzzer beater that went viral on the web with over 10,000 views and was picked up and shared by multiple websites. It’s a shot of Damien’s Jeremy Hemsley hitting a game-winning basket in the CIF-SS basketball playoffs.”
- Fred Robledo

Zen Vuong, Pasadena Star-News
“This Tout helps people better visualize the plight people with disabilities face at the South Pasadena Gold Line Station. It adds another layer to the lead character that starts this article.”
- Zen Vuong
