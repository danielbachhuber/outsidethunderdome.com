We’ve been enthusiastically building out our news team to provide more coverage for a longer part of the day. We’ve also added additional morning staff, when our audience is at its peak.

The news “producer” title has always been vague, at best. It is a role that means different things at different organizations, and even within the same organization. We look for a combination of attributes in producer candidates: Solid news judgment, good writing skills, deep familiarity with social news, an intense interest in the future of digital news, juggling skills (actual and/or metaphorical), with dashes of technical skills and experience with mediums like video.

We waded through many applications to find this recipe, and we think we hit on it with these new hires.

Meg Wagner has been with us for a couple weeks as a breaking news producer. She came to us from an internship at Mashable, and previously did an internship at Breakingnews.com. The experience she received there processing breaking news quickly was obviously a huge draw for us.

Follow Meg on Twitter at @meg_wagner or email her at mwagner@digitalfirstmedia.com

Jessica Glenza is working for the general news team as a news producer. She comes to us via our very own Register-Citizen of Torrington, Conn., where she covered high-profile stories like the Torrington rape case. Her Twitter feed is a living resume for deep interest in social news.

Follow Jessica on Twitter at @JessicaGlenza or email her at jglenza@digitalfirstmedia.com

Our new senior breaking news producer Kim Bui comes to us via KPCC, where she wore many hats, including social media editor and an interim mobile product manager for this KPCC iPad app. She’ll be staying in L.A. and working our night shift, which works out as a bit earlier for her on west coast time. She recently wrote about shifting to this new position after three years at KPCC.

Follow Kim on Twitter at @kimbui or email her at kbui@digitalfirstmedia.com

Jillian Sederholm has been with us for several months, but never got her appropriate Inside Thunderdome mention. Jillian was a social media editor at Newsday Westchester before joining our team as a breaking news producer, where she’s been doing an awesome job producing visual stories like this, and crafting social news stories by staying deeply embedded in social feeds and acting quickly on what she finds there. She is already a two-time winner in our headline of the week contest. 

Follow Jillian on Twitter at @JillianSed or email her at jsederholm@digitalfirstmedia.com

News organizations should always be thinking up new ways to crystallize stories and sum up perspectives on complex and ongoing issues. Take Quartz for example. Quartz (an Atlantic Media publication) has been aggressively covering the fiscal cliff negotiations in Washington, and recently put up a single-serving website to answer a simple question: Has the US gone off the fiscal cliff? Using a web cam pointed at a wall in its office and code pulled from a Google Doc, Quartz’s single-serving website is a quirky mashup of physical and digital that clearly reflects the site’s self-identity as a digitally native news outlet for the new global economy.
