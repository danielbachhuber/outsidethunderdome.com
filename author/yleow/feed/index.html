The votes are in for September’s Tout Bout! Congratulations to Catherine Bowen Mijs, Joel Rosenbaum, Greg Trott and Karen Robes Meeks, whose videos were voted the best of Digital First Media for the month of September.

Catherine Bowen Mijs, Joel Rosenbaum, and Greg Trott, from the Reporter in Vacaville, Calif., win the $200 prize for the best Tout shot on a mobile device. They beat out three other finalists in the mobile category with their “7-Alarm Fire” coverage taking 41 percent of the vote. Coming in second was “HS Football” by Robert Casillas of the Daily Breeze and Paula Mitchell of the Daily Freeman came in third with ”Car Accident.“

Karen Robes Meeks‘ “Boeing C-17″ won the $100 award for fantastic videos that were not shot and edited on a mobile device, but were distributed with Tout. This allows for some creative styling and advanced editing that just isn’t as easy on a mobile phone. Meeks, from the Long Beach Press-Telegram, earned 57 percent of the vote with her coverage of Boeing’s C-17 take-off from Long Beach airport.

Congratulations to all of the finalists!

Stay tuned to Inside Thunderdome for the announcement of our next Tout Bout. You can subscribe to our blog post feed on the right rail of the site, or follow us on Twitter). If you have a recent Tout you want to nominate now, go ahead, we’ll count them in the next Bout.

We are also looking for some journalists from Digital First Media to send to the Society of News Design 2013 workshop (SND) and NICAR in 2014. If you are interested, check the links for more information on how to enter.

It’s time for round two of Digital First Media’s monthly Tout Bout! We received some good suggestions from staff about last month’s Bout, so we’re mixing things up a bit this time around.

In our inaugural contest, we received a lot of nominations for really creative Touts that were filmed on video cameras and edited for distribution on Tout. Because of the notable differences in available features and quality, we didn’t feel these videos should compete with those Touts filmed on mobile devices.

With that in mind, there are now two categories for monthly Tout Bout submissions: 1) The most interesting Tout filmed with a mobile device and 2) The most interesting Tout not filmed on a mobile device. You can select which category your favorite Tout fits into in the nomination form below.

The winner of the most interesting Tout filmed on a mobile device will be awarded $200, and the winner of the most interesting non-mobile Tout will receive $100.

Because the non-mobile videos did not compete in July, those nominees will carry over to this month. The rest of the rules are essentially the same: Nominate your favorite Touts from the last month in the first round, we’ll narrow it down to the top 10, and then you vote for the winner in each category.

Remember that individuals can only win the Tout Bout up to three times a year. Reach out to Mandy Jenkins or myself (Yvonne Leow) if you have any thoughts, suggestions or questions about the contest.

So, lace up your gloves and roll your cameras because August’s Tout Bout officially begins now. Get some ideas by looking at last month’s entries and submit your Touts by Friday, August 16th!

Get your Touts ready because today we kick off the inaugural DFM Tout Bout, a monthly contest for Digital First staffers to nominate and ultimately choose the most creative, interesting or newsworthy Tout.

As Tout has been rolling out across the company, lots of DFMers have been experimenting with the tool and getting some great results. We want to highlight the best Touts out there – as chosen by you.

Nominate a colleague’s Tout – or your own – using the form below and get into the running for the monthly prize of $200. After the nomination process, we’ll narrow down the field to 10, and then YOU will vote for the winning Tout.

The Details

The prize: The creator of the best Tout each month will win $200.

How to enter a Tout: Nominate a Tout using the form below.

Then what? An unbiased group of judges will narrow the nomination to 10 finalists, which will be announced on Inside Thunderdome and Twitter on July 22. The monthly winner will be determined by a popular vote.  If you are a finalist, we’ll let you know via email so you can engage your network in the office and on social media to vote for you. Time to call in those favors!

Important dates: Though you can nominate a Tout all month along, nominations close for this month’s inaugural contest on July 17.

The top 10 finalists will be announced on July 22 and voting on the 10 finalists will run from July 22-26.

The winner for July will be announced on July 30. Then we start it all over again in August.

Restrictions: Individuals can only win the Tout Bout up to three times a year.

Questions? Reach out to Mandy Jenkins or me (Yvonne Leow) if you have any thoughts or suggestions.

You’ve already met the operations and data teams so allow me to introduce the video team. Next month, we’ll be welcoming a new member to the video team, but more on that later. In the meantime, here’s a little bit about me and what I do at Project Thunderdome.

My role is to develop a video strategy for DFM and to coordinate video coverage for our national channels (politics, sports, and the like). This includes streamlining production workflows, standardizing our video CMS, equipment, and software, identifying video partnerships, and helping our papers be more strategic about engaging new audiences and producing higher-quality videos. It’s not going to happen overnight, but it’s time we take video to the next level. It’s time our stories get seen. It’s time they made an impact. So how do we do it? It begins with leveraging our strengths. We have a lot of talent and expertise at DFM, but we need to start working together. We’re building a community of visual storytellers across the company, and this is what you can do to help:

  • If you’re not on it already, join the DFM Video google group. It’s the best place to swap ideas, ask questions and get feedback from other journos across the country.
  • Share your thoughts and give us your feedback. E-mail me or post it on the group. Let’s talk about how we can produce better video journalism because there’s a cost to being neutral.
  • Finally, subscribe to this blog for weekly DFM video updates. In the next post, I’ll be outlining this year’s video goals at DFM. You’ll want to read them.

With that being said, who am I? You can read my bio, or follow me on Twitter, but if you’re still curious, here are five fun facts about me. (Note: just remember #5.)

Five Fun Facts About DFM’s Video Editor Yvonne Leow 

1. Adventure. While studying abroad in Shanghai, I spent my birthday traveling to Beichuan, the epicenter of an 8.0 magnitude earthquake that struck the region six months before. It took a flight, three bus rides, and a made-up identity to get into the earthquake zone, but it was worth it.

2. Cambodia. My mom is from Cambodia, and my dad is from Singapore. As a result, I’m a big fan of coconut juice and durian shakes.

3. AP. I rarely give them this much credit, but my entertainment internship at the AP headquarters in NYC helped me decide to pursue a career in video journalism. Who wouldn’t enjoy interviewing Will Smith and watching his movies?

4. Startups. I tried to launch a media startup after I left the AP last year. It failed. But I still admire the life of an entrepreneur. It can be soul-crushing at times, but you genuinely fall in love with your work.

5. Pies. I’m a sucker for pies. To me, they’re better than birthday cake, especially the apple and pumpkin variety.
